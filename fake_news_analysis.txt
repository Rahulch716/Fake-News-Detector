================================================================================
                         FAKE NEWS PATTERNS
================================================================================

Fake news articles typically exhibit the following characteristics:

1. SENSATIONAL LANGUAGE
   - Excessive use of caps (SHOCKING, BREAKING, URGENT)
   - Multiple exclamation marks
   - Emotional triggers (fear, anger, excitement)
   - Words like "SECRET", "HIDDEN", "EXPOSED"

2. CLICKBAIT TECHNIQUES
   - Vague, attention-grabbing headlines
   - "They don't want you to know"
   - "You won't believe this"
   - Promise of hidden information

3. STRUCTURAL ELEMENTS
   - Short, punchy sentences
   - Poor readability scores
   - High special character ratio
   - Excessive punctuation

4. CONTENT CHARACTERISTICS
   - Conspiracy theories
   - Unverified claims
   - Emotional manipulation
   - Negative sentiment bias
   - Low entity density (few proper nouns)

5. EXAMPLES FROM TRAINING DATA
   ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   "SHOCKING discovery that changes everything! Doctors hate this!"
   "They don't want you to know this one simple trick for wealth!"
   "SECRET EXPOSED: What the elite don't want you to know!"
   "Wake up sheeple! The truth is being suppressed!"
   "This one weird trick will make you rich overnight!"
   ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
